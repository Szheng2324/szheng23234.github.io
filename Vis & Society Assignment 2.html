Change Over Time in Corporate Ownership and Owner Occupancy Rates in Boston Neighborhoods

Overall Analysis Questions

  1. How does the rate of owner-occupied properties and rate of corporate-owned properties change over time?
  2. What is the distribution of racial and age groups across the neighborhoods?
  3. What is the distribution of occupied and vacant housing across the neighborhoods?
  4. Who lives in the areas where change is happening the most and least?
  5. How many people do not live in owner-occupied properties?

Discoveries & Insights

Summary

In Boston, there are many neighborhoods that are all undergoing change in the number of houses being owned by private owners and large companies. In this analysis, we have identified the people who are affected the most. This includes age, race, and institutional living as well as the occupancy of units in different neighborhoods. There seems to be a slight correlation in race and rate of owner-occupied houses and almost no correlation in rate of corporate-owned. With this information, we cannot claim with full confidence how different people are being affected by the change in the housing market. To answer these questions in a more convincing way, we will need data like eviction and change in population over time.
